Maintaining a clean home is essential for comfort and health, but cleaning routines vary. Many homeowners get confused between a regular maintenance clean and a deep clean. Choosing the wrong service can lead to unmet expectations or unnecessary costs.

A regular clean is all about maintenance. It focuses on the surface-level cleaning tasks that keep your home tidy and sanitary week-to-week. This typically includes dusting surfaces, vacuuming and mopping floors, cleaning toilets and showers, and wiping down kitchen counters.

A deep clean goes much further. It targets the dirt, grime, and allergens that accumulate in hard-to-reach areas over time. Deep cleaning involves washing baseboards, scrubbing tile grout, cleaning inside appliances (oven, microwave, fridge), dusting light fixtures, wiping door frames, and sanitizing vents.

So, which do you need? You should schedule a deep clean if you are moving into a new home, moving out of an old one, preparing for a special event, or if your home hasn't been professionally cleaned in the last three months. Once a deep clean is completed, regular cleaning will easily keep the space fresh.
