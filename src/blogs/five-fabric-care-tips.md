In today’s fast-paced world, clothes can seem disposable. However, extending the life of your wardrobe is not only great for your wallet, but it is also a sustainable practice that helps reduce environmental waste. With simple changes to your laundry routine, you can keep your garments looking vibrant and fresh.

First, wash clothes inside out. This protects the outer fibers from friction against other clothes in the drum, reducing color fading and preventing pills from forming on the visible side of your shirts and trousers.

Second, pay attention to wash temperatures. Hot water breaks down fibers and makes dyes bleed. Whenever possible, wash your clothes in cold water. Modern detergents are designed to clean effectively in cold water, and it saves significant energy.

Third, avoid the dryer when possible. The heat and tumble action of a clothes dryer weaken fibers and shrink clothes. Air-drying on a rack or clothesline preserves elasticity and shape, giving clothes a much longer lifespan.

Fourth, separate your laundry properly. Don’t just separate lights and darks; also separate by fabric weight. Washing heavy items like jeans or towels with delicate t-shirts can cause friction damage to the lighter fabrics.

Finally, invest in high-quality hangers and storage. Store knits folded in a drawer rather than hanging them, as hanging stretches out the shoulders. For suits and coats, use wide wooden or padded hangers that support the shoulders’ structure.
